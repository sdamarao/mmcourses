3. Differences Highlighted

Some of the other differences between a conventional financial audit and an operational review are summarized in Exhibit 1.2.

Exhibit 1.2—Differences Between Financial Audits and Operational Reviews

  Characteristic Financial Audit     Operational Review    
1. Purpose Express opinion on financial condition Analyze and improve methods and performance
2. Scope Fiscal financial records Business operations
3. Skills Accounting Interdisciplinary
4. Time Orientation To the past To the future
5. Precision Absolute Relative
6. Audience Stockholders and the public Internal management
7. Necessity Legally required At option of management
8. Standards GAAP, GAAS, PCAOB, etc. Economy, efficiency, effectiveness
9. Opinion Required Not required
10. Audit results Opinion and financial statements Recommendations to management
11. Focus Financial statements presented fairly Operational positive improvements
12. Viewpoint Financial Management
13. Success Unqualified opinion Management adoption of recommendations
