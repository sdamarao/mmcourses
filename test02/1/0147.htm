Which is a technique for identifying critical problem areas?
 A. Review of financial records
 B. Review of organization charts
 C. Review of policies and procedures
 D. Identify key activities

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

The reviewer should be reviewing financial records during the planning phase; however, it is not the records themselves but the analyzing of such records that might result in the identification of a critical problem area. A critical problem area may be identified through noting key activities and making an analysis as to whether they help or hinder the effectiveness of operations.

The organization charts, if they exist, provide the reviewer with information relative to reporting relationships. Normally, they would not result in identifying a critical problem area but do constitute necessary documentation to be used in the fieldwork phase. A critical problem area may be identified through noting key activities and making an analysis as to whether they help or hinder the effectiveness of operations.

Policies and procedures document the expected manner in which operations should function, assuming they exist and are accurate. Rarely would they identify a critical problem area, although they may help identify roadblocks to effectiveness. A critical problem area may be identified through noting key activities and making an analysis as to whether they help or hinder the effectiveness of operations.

The correct answer is D.

A critical problem area may be identified through noting key activities and making an analysis as to whether they help or hinder the effectiveness of operations.
