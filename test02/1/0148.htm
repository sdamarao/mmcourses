What area should not be part of the review of administrative controls?
 A. Policies and procedures
 B. Accounting and other records
 C. Performance standards
 D. Compliance to GAAP and GAAS

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

Policies and procedures document the manner in which the area under review is expected to operate and hence is an integral part of administrative controls. The effectiveness and relevance of such policies and procedures is the important aspect in such a review.

Accounting and other records provide clear indications as to the manner in which the company operates as well as providing a good indication as to results. These records should absolutely be included in the review of administrative controls. Compliance to GAAP and GAAS would not part of the review of administrative controls.

Performance standards provide yardsticks for expected performance and provide some indication as to compliance to such standards. Although the reviewer might also be interested in testing the validity and reliability of any such performance standards as well as comparison of results to such standards, they definitely should be included in the review of administrative controls. Compliance to GAAP and GAAS would not part of the review of administrative controls.

The correct answer is D.

Compliance to GAAP and GAAS relates to the integrity of the financial records. While the reviewer might have some interest as to the company's compliance to such accounting standards, their main emphasis is on operations. Accordingly, such compliance would not part of the review of administrative controls.
