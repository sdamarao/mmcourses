Sample Planning Phase Organizational Work Program

1. Overview

Work steps that might be included in a planning phase operational review work program for the above organization might include:

(A)

Secure or prepare and organization chart with descriptions of each department's and work units' specific functions

(B)

Determine formal and informal reporting relationships from top to bottom, bottom to top, and across functional lines

(C)

Analyze actual operations to determine if such reporting is proper as to how the organization functions and whether it results in operational concerns and problems

(D)

Analyze each work unit's functions to determine whether they are appropriate

(E)

Document the duties and responsibilities of each employee. Obtain copies of existing job descriptions or prepare them through the use of user provided data such as a Job Responsibilities Questionnaire

(F)

Interview the president, vice presidents, managers and supervisors, and each employee to validate their functions

(G)

Observe actual work being performed to determine the necessity of all duties and responsibilities

(H)

Obtain or prepare company policies and procedures relating to each function under review

(I)

Determine that authority and responsibility relationships are clearly defined and understood by all personnel

(J)

Ascertain that all employees know their delegated authority and responsibilities; assure that the responsibilities are proper and do not overlap or duplicate another area

(K)

Look for functions and individuals that either are not providing value-added services or are not being cost effective. Examples may be isolates, dispatchers, controllers, unwieldy hierarchies typified by policing and control, and management that gets in the way

(L)

Review hiring, orientation, training, evaluation, promotion, and layoff/firing practices
