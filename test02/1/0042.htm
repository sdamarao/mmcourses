3. Specific Objectives

There are many reasons why management desires to have an operational review performed. Keep in mind that management may be looking for a single objective (e.g., operational efficiency), a combination of objectives (e.g., least cost but most efficient systems, best practices), or their own specific agenda (e.g., achievement of results on the basis of cost versus benefits). Some specific objectives are as follows:

(A). Financial and Accounting  

(1)

Adherence to financial policy

(2)

Performance of accounting procedures

(3)

Procedures performed by individuals with no incompatible functions

(4)

Adequate audit trail exists

(5)

Procedures can be observed

(6)

Personnel are adequately trained and know what they are doing

(7)

Internal controls are adequate but not unwieldy
