A source of information related to internal operations to be gathered in the planning phase includes which of the following?
 A. Financial data of other companies
 B. Detail interviews with operations staff
 C. Physical inspection of the operation
 D. Industry statistics

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

While financial of other companies, especially similar companies, might be useful information to acquire, it is not normally germane to internal operations. Physical inspection of the operation is a must activity in the planning phase and usually one of the initial activities.

As the major purpose of the planning phase is to gather information quickly to identify critical operational areas for inclusion in the review, primary interviewing in the planning phase would be with the key management personnel. Detail interviewing of operations staff takes place in the fieldwork phase. Physical inspection of the operation is a must activity in the planning phase and usually one of the initial activities.

Industry statistics might be an important element of information particularly when comparing the review entity to others in the industry; however, it would be included in the fieldwork phase. Physical inspection of the operation is a must activity in the planning phase and usually one of the initial activities.

The correct answer is C.

Physical inspection of the operation in the planning phase can provide a high degree of information about the function and activity such as under/over staffing, poor work flow and working conditions, uneven workloads, work atmosphere, poor work layout and so forth. It is a must activity in the planning phase and usually one of the initial activities.
