Operational reviews help to make which one of the following systems more helpful?
 A. Management bonus systems
 B. Planning and budgeting systems
 C. Employee evaluation systems
 D. Organizational downsizing

The purpose of an operational review is to ensure that the organization or function under review is operating in the most economical, efficient, and effective (the three E's of operational review) manner possible. While the reviewer may be asked to look at the management bonus system, it typically would be a top management issue and not included in an operational review; however, the criteria for granting such bonuses might be included in the review as to their relevance to the three E's.

Employee evaluation systems tend to be conducted in a subjective ineffective manner. Typically, the operational review would not delegate time to review this system; however, as part of the review, the reviewer may look at the impact such a system has on operating effectiveness and developing better practices to encourage employee performance and results. The budget system is looked at as the manifestation of the planning process by allocating scarce resources to the achievement of planning systems goals and objectives—encompassing the three E's.

Such procedures may result in the function being able to be performed with fewer personnel; however, it is not the reviewer's job to identify which individuals are to be replaced, this is management's job.
