1. Attributes

The progress and ultimate success in achieving the benefits of operational reviews depend greatly on the reviewers' skills and what management and others think of them. Those assigned to an operational review engagement must possess the ability to review and analyze financial, management, and operational areas. The attributes of an effective operational reviewer include the following:

(A)

Curiosity (imagination)

(B)

Analytical ability

(C)

Persuasiveness

(D)

Good business judgment

(E)

Common sense

(F)

Objectivity

(G)

Communication skills

(H)

Developing techniques such as work measurement, cost-benefit analysis, flowcharting, organizational analysis, IT, etc.

(I)

Independence

(J)

Confidence
