What is a primary purpose of the planning phase?
 A. To sell the concept of operational reviews to management
 B. To determine who should perform the review
 C. To identify possible problem areas
 D. To perform the work steps in the work program

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

While having support from management is important in ensuring the success of the operational review, it should have been accomplished prior to the start of the review. The importance of the planning phase is to gather and analysis as much information as possible about the area under review in a relatively short period so as to identify those critical problem areas to be included in the review and to begin to develop the work program.

It is important to determine the personnel to be involved in performing the actual review as the success of the review will only be as good as the skills and abilities of the personnel performing the review. This determination may, in fact, occur during the planning phase, but it is not a primary purpose of the planning phase.
