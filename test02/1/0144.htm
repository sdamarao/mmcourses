The 80/20 rule of operational reviews states which of the following?
 A. 80 percent of all activities are included in the operational review
 B. 80 percent of the problems impact 20 percent of the organization
 C. 80 percent of the problems impact 80 percent of the organization
 D. 20 percent of the problems impact 20 percent of the organization

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

Only those critical areas, usually around 20 percent of all activities, are included in the operational review. The 80/20 rule is that 20 percent of all activities (the elephants) cause 80 percent of all operational problems. It would be very rare that an entity is doing so poorly that 80 percent of its activities would be included in the review.

Eighty percent of the problems would not cause 80 percent of the impact. The 80/20 rule is that 20 percent of all activities (the elephants) cause 80 percent of all operational problems.

The 80/20 rule is that 20 percent of all activities cause 80 percent of all operational problems. Twenty percent of the problems would very rarely cause only 20 percent of the operational impact.

The correct answer is B.

The 80/20 rule is that 20 percent of all activities (the elephants) cause 80 percent of all operational problems. These are the critical activities that are to be included in the review. Conversely, 80 percent of all activities (the mice) cause 20 percent of the problems. This is, of course, a rule of thumb; but this relationship usually works fairly close in reality.
