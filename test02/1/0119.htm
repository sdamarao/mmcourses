1. Factors

The task of appraising the effectiveness of internal reports involves determination of the following three factors:

(A)

The purpose of the report or the primary communication management intends the report to perform (the intended function)

(B)

The way in which the report affects employees (the actual function)

(C)

The correlation or degree of correspondence between the report's intended effect and the actual effect on the function

2. Function

The reviewer's task is to determine that the intended function and the actual function (how the report's recipient perceives her/his function) are the same. If they are not the same, there is a lack of effective communication. If, on the other hand, such reports are not used, inquiry can be made as to the methods by which control is exercised, which may point to a lack of effective control, with related laxity and administrative weakness.
