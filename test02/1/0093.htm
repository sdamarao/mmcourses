What is one of the major purposes of an operational review?
 A. Identify opportunities for retrenchment
 B. Assess performance
 C. Evaluate management and personnel performance
 D. Develop recommendations for personnel discipline

There is more than one correct answer.

The purpose of the operational review is to identify opportunities for improvements, not for retrenchment; however, it is possible that the reviewer may develop a recommendation for improvement that results in retrenchment or the lessening of operations. Managers at all levels should be held accountable for using the scarce resources entrusted to them to achieve maximum results at the least possible costs.

It is not the primary purpose in an operational review to evaluate management and personnel performance. The focus should be on whether they are achieving maximum results with the level of resources entrusted to them and if not achieving those results what are the factors impeding such achievement. However, there is the possibility that such an evaluation of personnel performance may become a by-product of the review in instances such as sub-par or detrimental performance. Managers at all levels should be held accountable for using the scarce resources entrusted to them to achieve maximum results at the least possible costs.

While a proper product emanating from the operational review is the construct of proper and reasonable workable recommendations, their purpose is to improve operations (hopefully through the implementation of best practices) not for disciplining specific employees. In fact, such an approach would most likely be detrimental to the success of the review. The correct answer is B.

The operational review focuses on evaluating the effective use of scarce resources that maximize results. As part of this review, the reviewer must be able to effectively assess performance as to the achievement of desired results.
