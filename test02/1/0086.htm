Which one is not an attribute that the successful operational reviewer need possess?
 A. The skill to effectively communicate results
 B. Ability to render an opinion on the fairness of the financial statements
 C. Good common business sense and judgment
 D. Ability to spot the trouble areas

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

The skill to effectively communicate the results of an operational review, both orally and written, is one of the most important attributes that the reviewer possess. Even the most successful operational review may garner minimal response if the reviewer cannot effectively convince and persuade management to take action. It is not the responsibility of the operational reviewer to render an opinion on the fairness of the financial statements.

As the goal of the operational review is to review activities with an eye towards developing best practices for each function and activity reviewed, the reviewer should posses the attribute of good common business sense and judgment. Another operational review rule is that the reviewer should possess 20% technical ability and 80% good common business sense. The quality of the results of the review is only as good as the ability of the reviewer to exercise quality judgment. It is not the responsibility of the operational reviewer to render an opinion on the fairness of the financial statements.

As each review is dependent upon the personnel performing the review, the results of the review will vary based on the expertise and perception of the reviewer. As one of the first steps in developing those areas to include in the review is the identity of the company's most critical areas, the attribute or ability to spot the trouble spots will determine to which extent the review chases elephants as opposed to milking mice. It is not the responsibility of the operational reviewer to render an opinion on the fairness of the financial statements.

The correct answer is B.

It is not the responsibility of the operational reviewer to render an opinion on the fairness of the financial statements.
