Chapter 1. Introduction to Operational Reviews

Upon completion of this chapter, the user should be able to:

  • identify operational areas in need of positive improvement—looking for best practices as part of a program for continuous improvements,

  • pinpoint the cause (not the symptom) of the problem—avoiding quick fix short-term solutions in favor of longer term elegant solutions,

  • quantify the effect of the present situation on operations—identifying the cost of present practices and the benefits to be derived through implementation of best practices, and

  • develop recommendations as to alternative courses of action to correct the situation—identifying best practices in a program of continuous improvements.
