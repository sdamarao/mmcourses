(D). Review Techniques  

The operational review requires the reviewer to possess a number of varied tools and techniques. The following are included among these review techniques:

(1)

Planning and budget processes

(2)

Cost analysis; such as direct, standard, and activity-based costing methods

(3)

Preparation and analysis of systems flowcharts

(4)

Development and/or analysis of computer systems and programs

(5)

Evaluation of computer processing procedures and results

(6)

Statistical sampling procedures

(7)

Development and understanding of forecasts and projections

(8)

Interviewing skills and communication skills; both oral and written

(9)

Organizational planning development and analysis

(10)

Creation of goals and objectives and other performance standards of measurement

(11)

Development and analysis of organizational structures

(12)

Identification of best practices—both internal and external—and development of a program of continuous improvements

(13)

Determination of compliance with laws and regulations

(14)

Use of sophisticated analytical techniques such as matrix analysis, linear regression correlation, PERT, critical path method and so on

(15)

Cost/benefit analysis

(16)

Knowledge of current thinking and procedures, such as Total Quality Management (TQM), benchmarking, reengineering, and Complexity Theory
