2. Mental Models And Belief Systems

Many organizations operate on the basis of prevalent mental models or belief systems—usually emanating from past and present top management—which have an overriding effect on the conditions with which operations within the company are carried out. They can help to produce a helpful working environment or atmosphere or a hindering one. In effect, such mental models become performance drivers—those elements within the organization that shape the direction of how employees will perform their functions. Examples of such mental models and belief systems include the following:

(A)

Hard work and doing what you are told are the keys to success for the individual and the company.

(B)

The obedient child in the company survives and is promoted, while the rebellious child is let go or leaves the company.

(C)

Only managers can make decisions.

(D)

Power rises to the top—and stays there.

(E)

Employees need to be watched to do their jobs.

(F)

Power and control over employees is necessary to get results.
