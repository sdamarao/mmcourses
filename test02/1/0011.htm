Organizational Basics

1. Criteria For Organizational Growth

There are numerous criteria organizations may choose to implement in their programs of continuous improvement leading toward organizational growth. As part of conducting an operational review, the reviewer must be aware of these criteria to be successful in addressing the company's desired direction—in total or by business segment or function. Some of these criteria include the following:

(A)

Cost Reductions

(B)

Price Increases

(C)

Sales Volume Increases

(D)

New Market Expansion

(E)

New Distribution Channels

(F)

Market Share Increase in Existing Markets

(G)

Selling or Closing a Losing Operation or Location

(H)

Acquire Another Company, Division, Operation, or Product

(I)

Developing a New Product or Service

(J)

Efficiency or Productivity Improvements

(K)

Non-Value-Added Activities Eliminated

(L)

Making Employees Responsible

(M)

Organizational Structure Revisions
