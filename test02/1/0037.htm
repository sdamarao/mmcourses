Financial Audits Versus Operational Reviews

1. Differences Between Financial Audits and Operational Reviews

To illustrate the differences between financial audits and operational reviews—using operational review concepts—the reviewer is less concerned with determining whether purchase requisitions and orders and suppliers' invoices reflect proper approvals, as in a financial audit, but more concerned with such operational aspects as whether:

(A)

The materials were really needed

(B)

Quantities used or purchased were reasonable

(C)

There was avoidable waste and exposure to damage or loss

(D)

Requisitioners exercised undue influence over purchasing by designating sources of purchase
