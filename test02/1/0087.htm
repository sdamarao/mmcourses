Study Question

Select the best answer.

What are the three Es or purposes of operational reviews?
 A. Evaluation, engineering, and economy
 B. Efficiency, extensive, and effort
 C. Economy, effectiveness, and expectations
 D. Economy, efficiency, and effectiveness

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

While evaluation is an important analytical component of appraising operations and results it is a technique used in the review and not the purpose for the review. Engineering of various sorts (i.e., work measurement, production standards, data collection) may be incorporated into review work steps, but again is not the purpose for the review. Economy is one of the three Es. Efficiency and effectiveness are the two remaining “Es.”

Although efficiency is one of the three Es, extensive and excellence are not. They are merely descriptors of other attributes such as extensive marketing reach and excellence in customer service. Economy and effectiveness are the two remaining “Es.”

Again, economy and effectiveness are two of the elements of the three Es; however, expectations might be an important element as to management and the reviewer's desires as to the results of the review it is not a purpose for the review. Efficiency is the last of the three “Es.”

The correct answer is D.

The purpose for conducting the operational review is to appraise the economy, efficiency, and effectiveness of the organization and those areas selected for review. These three purposes are at the crux of performing the operational review and must be continually evaluated by the reviewer during each step of the review.
