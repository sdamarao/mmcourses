Material on the Organization

The second area in which the reviewer needs to gather information involves the organization and its activities. Primary emphasis should be on the activities that are within the scope of the operational review. This information should include the following:

  1. Division of duties and responsibilities

  2. Principal delegations of authority

  3. Nature, size, and location of each operational entity (that is, any field offices)

  4. Number of employees by organization segment and location

  5. Nature and location of physical assets and accounting records

note

Among the reviewer's primary concerns is to determine how the company is organized to carry out its functions and how duties and responsibilities are assigned. In addition, the reviewer must determine where the area being reviewed fits into the organizational pattern of the whole company and its relationship to other areas. This knowledge is necessary for a full understanding of the organization's operations.
