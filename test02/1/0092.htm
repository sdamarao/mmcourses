A criteria for determining a company's critical areas could include?
 A. Areas subject to abuse and laxity
 B. Areas not performing as well as competitors
 C. Areas where management has identified specific weaknesses
 D. Areas where compensating controls exist

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

Although an area that does not perform as well as competitors could be included as part of the operational review, there is a possibility that the area still functions more than adequately and is not all that critical in overall operations. A critical area would be an area subject to abuse or laxity.

Many times the reviewer will include those areas that management has identified with specific weaknesses even though it may not be a critical area. This approach helps to ensure the cooperation of operational management and may facilitate the acceptance of recommendations. A critical area would be an area subject to abuse or laxity.

The reviewer may include those areas where controls are weak but if there are effective compensating controls they might exclude it as a critical area. A critical area would be an area subject to abuse or laxity.

The correct answer is A.

As the operational review attempts to focus on those areas creating the most significant problems (i.e., the 20% of all activities that cause 80% of the operational problems), areas subject to abuse and laxity such as storeroom operations, returned merchandise, rejects and rework, are always considered.
