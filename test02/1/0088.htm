What is the major purpose of an operational review?
 A. To be critical of present operations
 B. To review operations for positive improvements
 C. To identify areas for reduction and elimination
 D. To review operations for the existence of adequate controls

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

The review team to be most successful must perform the operational review in a helping atmosphere working together with operations personnel to ensure achieving maximum results. Taking a critical viewpoint of operations tends to put operational personnel on the defensive, increase resistance to cooperate with the reviewers, establishes an adversary relationship and minimizes the chance of acceptance of best practice recommendations. Using the three Es of operational review as the guiding light in the performance of the review, the expected result is for all operations under review to be improved in a positive manner directed toward best practices in a program of continuous improvements.

While the identification of areas for reduction and elimination may become a result of the operational review, it is very rarely a major purpose. Should this be the case, it would be extremely difficult to gain the cooperation of those operational personnel involved in the function under review. Using the three Es of operational review as the guiding light in the performance of the review, the expected result is for all operations under review to be improved in a positive manner directed toward best practices in a program of continuous improvements.

Unlike a specific review of internal controls, both financial and operational, the operational review is less concerned as to adequate controls but more concerned as to the economy, efficiency, and effectiveness of operations. Should the reviewer identify a weakness or absence of adequate controls during the course of the review, they should certainly bring such a condition to the attention of management. The correct answer is B.
