The following median multiples developed through the use of guideline transactions were established for the subject company in order to calculate potential values for the company being valued:

Multiple used Potential value calculated
MVIC/Sales $375,000
MVIC/EBITDA $510,000
Price/Earnings $710,000

The business valuator would likely make what decision related to value using the guideline transaction method?
 A. Determine that the guideline transaction method did not provide reasonable results.
 B. Value the subject company at $375,000.
 C. Value the subject company at $510,000.
 D. Value the subject company at $710,000.

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

Although this lowest calculated result would comply with the accounting standard of conservatism, this standard is not particularly useful in business valuation.

Although this is the median of the three calculated values, the valuator is not likely to use this average value as it is not comparable.

Although the potential seller of this organization might appreciate a high value, the business valuator would not likely be able to defend this calculation due to the wide dispersion of results using the industry multiples.

The correct answer is A.

Per the Delaware Chancery Court, a broad range of values resulting from comparable analysis is too unreliable to be given significant weight and should not be used in such a case.
