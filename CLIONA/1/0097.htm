Which of the following statements about the NAICS code is false?
 A. NAICS codes are extremely general in nature and not helpful to a business valuator.
 B. NAICS codes can be a starting point for determining comparability.
 C. NAICS codes replaced the prior SIC (Standard Industrial Classification) system.
 D. NAICS codes were developed in a cooperative effort by the U.S., Canada, and Mexico.

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

NAICS codes can be a good starting point for determining comparability between the subject company and possible guideline organizations since a limited number of companies can be established from which possible guideline organization can be found.

NAICS codes did replace SIC coding system in 1997.

NAICS codes were developed by the United States, Canada, and Mexico so that there would be a significant degree of comparability in various business statistics within North America.

The correct answer is A.

Although the NAICS codes provide only general information, they do help the business valuator narrow the number of possible guidelines to review.
