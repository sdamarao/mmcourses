Finding a Guideline Company

North American Industry Classification System Codes

A good starting point when searching for guideline companies is to determine the North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) code for the firm being valued. These codes are used by various Federal statistical agencies for the function of collecting, analyzing, and distributing statistical data related to numerous industries throughout North America. Replacing the Standard Industrial Classification (SIC) system in 1997, NAICS codes were developed by the United States, Canada, and Mexico so that there would be a significant degree of comparability in various business statistics within North America. The U.S. NAICS Manual can be extremely helpful in that it contains definitions of each industry as well as potentially valuable background information. The official U.S. Government website (http://www.census.gov/cgi-bin/sssd/naics/naicsrch) provides useful and current information related to the available NAICS references and tools.
