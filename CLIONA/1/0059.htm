Harriet, CPA, has been engaged to prepare a business valuation for a local gear company. During her initial inquiries, she discovered that a liquidation of the company assets is likely. In such a situation, which of the following would the business valuation would likely focus on?
 A. Expected future earnings.
 B. Fair market value of the assets.
 C. Liquidation value of the assets.
 D. Replacement value of the assets.

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

Since there will be no future earnings in liquidation, a business would not focus on expected future earnings.

Fair market value is based on the highest and best use of the property, and it is unlikely that the best use of the property is liquidation.

Since the company is liquidating, assets will not be replaced, and replacement value of the assets is not important.

The correct answer is C.

The value of the net assets would be the focus of the valuation due to the lack of potential future income flows. Liquidation value would be the lowest value that a seller would be willing to accept.
