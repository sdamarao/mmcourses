Study Question

Select the best answer.

Which of the following is not an important element in determining fair market value?
 A. Both parties must have reasonable knowledge of the relevant facts.
 B. Neither party is under the compulsion to complete the transaction.
 C. The parties are aware of the elements of Revenue Ruling 59-60.
 D. The predicted future is neither unreasonably pessimistic nor optimistic.

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

If one party has relevant knowledge that the other does not, then a level playing field does not exist.

Since it is important that neither party to the transaction has an advantage, it is necessary that neither party feels forced to complete the transaction in question.

Because the predicted future cannot be guaranteed, fair market value assumes that the prediction of said future is not unrealistically skewed in either direction.

The correct answer is C.

The definition of fair market value does not require that both parties are aware of the elements of Revenue Ruling 59-60, which defines a closely held corporation.
