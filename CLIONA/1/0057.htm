Which of the following does the asset based approach to business valuation utilize?
 A. Additional income created by the intangible assets above what would be expected to be normal income created by the net tangible asset of a company as part of the determination of value.
 B. Discounted future net cash inflows of a going concern to determine the value of a closely held business.
 C. The fair market value of operating and nonoperating assets to determine the value of a closely held business.
 D. The use of guideline companies to determine the value of a closely held business.

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

An excess-income approach focuses on the additional income that is produced by intangible assets.

The income approach is primarily concerned with valuing businesses that are expected to have positive future income-producing capacity.

The market approach uses market comparisons involving the use of guideline companies to determine the value of a closely held business.

The correct answer is C.

An asset-based approach utilizes the fair market value of operating and nonoperating assets to determine the value of a closely held business. An asset-based approach is an excellent example of what is known as the “economic substitution principle.” It states that no one would be willing to pay more for an asset than the price to obtain a substitute.
