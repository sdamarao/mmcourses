2 A. Use of Guidelines for Valuation

Guidelines

Recall the previous discussion of the provisions of Revenue Ruling 59-60 and Regulation Sections 20.2031-1(b) and 25.2512-1, which provided, respectively, a definition of a closely held business and of the concept of fair market value for the purposes of valuing a closely held business. These sources suggested that when dealing with the valuation of a closely held firm for estate- and gift-tax purposes, the IRS and the courts prefer that the valuation be based on historical facts rather than projections. In this context, it is desirable that the valuation be based on the use of guideline companies (formerly known as comparables).

In this chapter, we will deal with the key issues associated with finding and using guideline companies in the valuation process as well as discuss the basic analytical issues involved in a business valuation when using a market method.
