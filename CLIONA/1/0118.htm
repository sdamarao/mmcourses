Study Question

Select the best answer.

It is often difficult to find guideline companies. What is a key problem in finding such comparables for a small, closely held business?
 A. Most small, closely held firms cannot get credit.
 B. Most small, closely held firms do not fit within a NAICS classification.
 C. Most small, closely held firms do not have direct competitors.
 D. Most small, closely held firms lack depth of management.

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

Small firms have credit available, but they may not have as many sources of credit as larger corporations.

All companies fit into a NAICS classification.

Small firms often do have competitors; however, a guideline company does not have to be a direct competitor of the subject organization in order to be considered to have reasonable comparability.

The correct answer is D.

Most small, closely held firms do not have the depth of management that larger corporations have. Should something happen to the manager of a small business, the operation may not be able to continue.
