Business Valuation is a niche that is growing in importance within many accounting practices. How can business valuation be defined?
 A. A process by which a monetary value is established for an entire or partial interest in a company
 B. A process clearly spelled out by Revenue Ruling 59-60 for determining a value for a corporation
 C. A process that focuses upon the par value of the stock of a closely held corporation to determine the value of that company
 D. A process that uses the book value of a company's assets to determine the value of the subject company

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

Revenue Ruling 59-60 defines a closely held corporation, and discusses approaches to valuation and factors to consider when determining a value for such an organization. Since not all corporations are closely held, Revenue Ruling 59-60 does not apply to all corporations.

The par value does not take into consideration the fair market value of the stock. Par value is the arbitrary initial selling price and is not used in business valuation.

The book value contains the historical, depreciated cost items and could result in an unreasonable valuation.

The correct answer is A.

Valuation of a closely held business is the process by which a monetary value is established for an entire or partial interest in that business.
