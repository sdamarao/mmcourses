Thus, while the statute requires that firms simply be in a similar line of business to be considered a guideline company, the IRS indicates that this “similarity” must extend to other factors as well.

There are two basic approaches for using guideline companies.

The guideline public company method which is:

A method within the market approach whereby market multiples are derived from the prices of stock of companies that are engaged in the same or similar lines of business and that are actually traded on the free and open market (International Glossary of Business Valuation Terms, June 2001)

The guideline company transaction method which is:

The market approach whereby market multiples are derived from transactions of significant interest in companies engaged in the same or similar lines of business (International Glossary of Business Valuation Terms, June 2001)
