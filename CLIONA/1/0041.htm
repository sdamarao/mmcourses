Which of the following is true when the standard of value used in a particular business valuation prepared as an aid for the sale of a closely held firm is fair market value?
 A. The price the buyer is willing to pay and the seller is willing to accept is determined
 B. The use of fair market value demonstrates the objectivity of the valuator
 C. The valuation is done to comply with Regulation Section 25.2512-1
 D. The value may serve as a guide to the buyer and seller as they negotiate their price

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

The buyer and seller do not have to use the fair market value as a selling price even though it has been used in the valuation process.

Objectivity is demonstrated by the valuator's impartiality and lack of conflict of interest and not by the use of a particular standard of value.

Regulation Section 25.2512-1 defines fair market value. It does not provide compliance regulations that must be followed.

The correct answer is D.

The fair market value can be considered a launch point for negotiations between the buyer and seller. They will not necessarily arrive at a mutual agreement based solely on the fair market value determined in the process of the valuation engagement.
