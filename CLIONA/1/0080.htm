Which of the following terminology is correct within the context of business valuation?
 A. Justifiable similarity means that the valuator must be able to defend his/her choices of guidelines to other informed individuals.
 B. Logical similarity means that the company being valued and the guideline company are similar in a number of characteristics.
 C. Reasonable similarity means that the valuator must be able to defend his/her choices of guidelines to other informed individuals.
 D. Understandable similarity means that the valuator will be able to easily explain his/her choices of guidelines to other, less informed individuals.

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

Logical similarity is not a term used in business valuation, but the term is used when discussing the architecture of microprocessors.

Reasonable similarity means that the company being valued and the guideline companies are similar in a number of characteristics.

Understandable similarity is not a term used in business valuation. Although a valuator may need to be able to explain his/her choice of guidelines to those who are not experts in business valuation, the valuator does not need to make decisions based on a lower level of knowledge.

The correct answer is A.

Justifiable similarity does mean that the valuator could defend his/her choices of comparables to other informed individuals. The comparable must be both justifiably and reasonably similar to the business being valued.
