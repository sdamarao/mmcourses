Although the courts favor the use of a market valuation method, the use of the Guideline Transaction method can lead to misleading, distorted, or meaningless results in a business valuation for all of the reasons EXCEPT which of the following?
 A. The potential guideline transaction involved a company that had sales between 10% and 15% higher than the subject company for the past 5 years.
 B. The purchase in a potential guideline transaction occurred between related parties.
 C. The purchaser in a potential guideline transaction gained synergy through the purchase of a small business.
 D. The seller in a potential guideline transaction also accepted a non-compete agreement as part of the sale.

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

When a purchase occurs between related parties, the purchase price is often below market value; thus, reliance upon such a transaction could lead to an abnormally low valuation.

Synergy can result in a greater value for the purchaser than what would be expected in the normal market; therefore, the purchaser in such a situation may have paid more than the normal market value for the business that was purchased.

The purchase price should actually be divided between the price paid for the business in this transaction and the non-compete agreement. A valuation using the total price in this potential guideline transaction could result in a distorted result.

The correct answer is A.

Although size can be important when determining whether a company is a good fit when using a market valuation method, a company with sales 10% to 15% higher would not necessarily preclude the transaction from being excluded for use when using the guideline transaction method.
