Revenue Ruling 59-60 provides guidance for the business valuator and provides a list of factors that are fundamental to the valuation process and require careful analysis in all valuations. Which of the following factors should not be considered in a valuation?
 A. The general economic outlook as well as the outlook for the specific industry of which the subject company is part
 B. The percentage of closely held businesses to publicly traded companies within the industry
 C. The subject company's earnings capacity
 D. The subject company's nature and history from inception

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

Economic factors can give the valuator insight into the future value of the business being valued and should be considered when preparing a business valuation.

Since future benefits that might be provided by the subject company are important to the valuation process, earning capacity must be considered when preparing a business valuation.

Historical factors as well as the character of a business can serve as indicators for how the company will perform in the future. A valuator must remember, however, to ignore historical events that are unlikely to recur.

The correct answer is B.

The percentage of closely held businesses to publicly traded companies does not assist the valuator in making a meaningful comparison. There are other, more relevant factors that must be carefully considered.
