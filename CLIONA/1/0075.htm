The Dividend-Paying Capacity

Unlike publicly held companies, the dividend-paying capacity of a closely held organization may not be the same as the dividends actually paid. It is not unusual for a family held company to base dividend payments on the cash needs of the owners and/or their wish to avoid double taxation. Therefore, it is not a good practice to base the dividend-paying capacity on past practices when valuing a closely held company. The decision to pay dividends, increase salaries, or pay bonuses are at the discretion of the majority stockholder(s).

Care must be taken when reviewing the net income level and dividend payments. It is not unusual for a closely held organization to manipulate earnings and dividend levels to obtain desired results. For example, in order to avoid the double taxation of dividends, the majority owner of a small business can increase the officer's salary or bonus in order to keep the equity level at a specified debt to equity balance by not paying dividends.
