Since inventories are purchased for the purpose of obtaining a return, it is extremely important to determine if corrective action needs to be taken. Excessive inventories can lead to high storage and insurance costs, a greater risk of obsolescence, physical deterioration, or theft, tying up funds that could be used in a more productive fashion. This leads to decreased profits

Different inventory cost flow assumptions (FIFO vs. LIFO) can produce widely different inventory valuations and, thus, different turnover ratios. This can also lead to comparison problems between organizations. Therefore, when comparing the subject company to possible guidelines, it is important to be sure that the data is normalized, if necessary, to provide the same cost flow assumption for the companies being compared.
