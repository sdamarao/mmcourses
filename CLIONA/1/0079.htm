How can guideline organizations, formerly known as comparables, be defined?
 A. Any firm operating within the same geographic region as the company being valued
 B. Any firm that is in the same industry as the firm being valued
 C. Any organization that has the same NAICS classification as the firm being valued
 D. One that has a reasonable and justifiable similarity to the firm being valued

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

Being in the same region may be important when searching for a guideline; however, this criterion does not define a guideline company.

Being in the same industry as the company being valued is not the sole criterion to define a guideline although such a connection would help to narrow the pool of available business when searching for guideline companies.

The NAICS classification may be a useful tool to help aid in the search for guideline companies to use. However, it is by no means the complete definition of a guideline company.

The correct answer is D.

Reasonable and justifiable similarity means that the guideline must be similar to the company being valued in a number of ways, and the valuator can defend her choice of guidelines to other informed individuals.
