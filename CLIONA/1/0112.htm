Study Question

Select the best answer.

Finding guideline companies has been made easier through the use of currently available databases. Which of the following does not demonstrate this ease of research?
 A. Many databases provide easy access to financial reports.
 B. Ratio information is often provided by NAICS classification.
 C. Some databases have the capability of developing individual and aggregate ratios for any group of firms desired.
 D. Terms are defined in the same manner by all databases.

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

Many databases allow the user to find pertinent financial data quickly. The information in these databases can be sorted electronically by relevant factors. In addition, it is relatively easy to generate ratios and reports from those databases.

Many databases have the capacity to develop individual and aggregate ratios for a chosen group of firms or by industry.

Morningstar's U.S. Equities is an example of a database with this capability.

The correct answer is D.

Unfortunately, various ratios may not be calculated in the identical from one database to another. Also, inventory valuation or depreciation methods may differ between the subject company and potential guideline organization making comparison difficult.
