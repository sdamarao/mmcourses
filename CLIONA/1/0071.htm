When examining the balance sheet, the business valuator needs to determine whether there are any non-operating assets listed. If so, such items should be removed from the balance sheet for the valuation of the basic operations, revalued at market value, and added back to the estimated value determined for the operations of the company.

If there is more than one class of stock, it is important for the business valuator to determine:

the voting powers of the various classes,

the dividend preferences (if any), and

liquidation preferences (if any).
