Tax-Related Valuations

It is important to remember that tax-related valuations (estate and gift taxes) use the definition of fair market value that sets price at a level “at which the property would change hands between a willing buyer and a willing seller when the former is not under any compulsion to buy and the latter is not under any compulsion to sell” (Revenue Ruling 59-60 , §2.02). The valuations done for tax purposes also tend not to focus on future earnings since such returns are considered difficult to predict and speculative in nature. Thus, the focus is on the use of comparables.
