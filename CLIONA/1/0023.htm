Study Question

Select the best answer.

The definition of fair market value as set forth in Regulation Section 20.2031-1(b) and Regulation Section 25.2512-1 includes all of following components except which one?
 A. That both parties have reasonable knowledge of relevant facts
 B. The fact that neither the buyer nor the seller is under any compulsion to buy or sell
 C. The opportunity cost of the assets
 D. The price at which the property would change hands between a willing buyer and seller

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

Regulation Section 20.2031-1(b) and Regulation Section 25.2512-1 state that both parties have reasonable knowledge of relevant facts. Furthermore, neither party should have an advantage over the other in terms of the relevant facts of the transaction; a level playing field is assumed when determining fair market value.

If one of the parties of the transaction is being forced to buy or sell, the value is unlikely to reflect the fair market value which would be determined when both parties are not under any compulsion to engage in the transaction.

Regulation Section 20.2031-1(b) and Regulation Section 25.2512-1 define fair market value as the price at which the property would change hands between a willing buyer and a willing seller. Remember that the key here is “willing.” If one party is not a willing buyer or seller (as in the case of a divorce), the actual price likely will not reflect fair market value.

The correct answer is C.

Opportunity costs are the foregone return from alternative choices that are not selected. While opportunity cost does deal with a hypothetical cost, it is not addressed in either of the regulations in the question, and is not an element of fair market value.
