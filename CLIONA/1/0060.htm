Charles, CPA, has just been contacted by the Majestic Company to perform a business valuation of the entire organization. What is the starting point for this business valuation?
 A. To check with the IRS to see what valuation method must be used.
 B. To determine the purpose of the valuation.
 C. To hire a qualified practitioner to provide a value for equipment owned and used by the subject firm.
 D. To make sure that the firm's accounting records comply with GAAP.

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

The IRS does not determine what method is used in a business valuation.

Although hiring a qualified practitioner would be important in many valuations, it is not the starting point for a valuation.

Since GAAP financial statements are not necessarily used in a valuation, this would not be the starting point for a valuation.

The correct answer is B.

Different purposes for a valuation will suggest different valuation methods to use, which will, in turn, result in different values.
