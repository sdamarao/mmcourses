What is the definition of going-concern value?
 A. The net amount that would be realized if the business is terminated and the assets are sold piecemeal.
 B. The price for which a business would exchange hands given a willing buyer and a willing seller who are both knowledgeable about the relevant facts of the transaction, are under no compulsion to enter into the transaction, and have no special relationship.
 C. The value of a business enterprise that is expected to continue to operate into the future.
 D. The value that an investor considers, on the basis of an evaluation or available facts, to be the “true” or “real” value that will become the market value when other investors reach the same conclusion.

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

This is the definition of liquidation value per the International Glossary of Business Valuation Terms.

This is a definition of fair market value per the International Glossary of Business Valuation Terms.

This is a definition of intrinsic value per the International Glossary of Business Valuation Terms.

The correct answer is C.

Going concern value is the value of a business enterprise that is expected to continue to operate into the future. The value created in a company as the result of intangibles such as an expert workforce or a loyal, established customer base, are part of the going concern value.
