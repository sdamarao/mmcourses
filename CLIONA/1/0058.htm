What does the market approach potentially use _____ to determine the value of a business?
 A. A search for guideline companies
 B. The normal income created by the net tangible assets of the company
 C. The FMV (fair market value) of the assets less the FMV of the liabilities of a company
 D. The future earnings capacity of the adjusted net assets

You must select an answer.
There is more than one correct answer.

The normal income created by the net tangible assets of the company and the excess income created by the intangible assets of the company are used in the excess-income approach.

The asset approach focuses on the FMV of the net assets of an organization as a basis of value. This approach requires that each of the GAAP-basis assets and liabilities must be examined and restated if necessary to market, replacement, or liquidation value.

When utilizing an income approach to business valuation, future earnings are important. However, the market approach does not focus on future earnings.

The correct answer is A.

The market approach to valuation requires a search for comparables (guideline) organizations. This valuation method is preferred by the IRS and courts, so a search for comparables should be performed.
