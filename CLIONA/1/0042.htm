1 D.

Methods of Valuation

reminder

The starting point in preparing a business valuation is to determine the purpose of that valuation.

In valuing a going concern, future income may be a factor. In valuing a business that is liquidating, the value of the net assets would be the focus of the valuation due to the lack of potential future income flows. If the valuation is for estate or gift-tax purposes, the courts generally prefer verifiable facts to projections. Such facts would include the fair market value of the assets involved and past income.

An individual purchasing a closely held business would be interested in the value of the intended use of the assets, which might not necessarily be their current use.
