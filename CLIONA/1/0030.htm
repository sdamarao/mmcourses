Independence would be impaired if a member performs an appraisal, valuation, or actuarial service for an attest client where the results of the service, individually or in the aggregate, would be material to the financial statements and the appraisal, valuation, or actuarial service involves a significant degree of subjectivity.

(American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, Code of Professional Conduct, ET Section 1.295.110.02 – Independence, Nonattest Services, Appraisal, Valuation and Actuarial Services)

Examples of valuations that generally involve a significant degree of subjectivity include, ESOPs, business combinations, or appraisals of assets or liabilities. Accordingly, if these services produce results that are material to the attest client's financial statements, independence would be impaired. Valuations performed in connection with, for example, employee stock ownership plans, business combinations, or appraisals of assets or liabilities generally involve a significant degree of subjectivity. Accordingly, if these services produce results that are material to the financial statements, independence would be impaired.

(American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (April 30, 2014), Code of Professional Conduct, ET Section 101 1.295.110.04 – Independence, .05 101-3 Nonattest Services, Appraisal, Valuation and Actuarial Services)
