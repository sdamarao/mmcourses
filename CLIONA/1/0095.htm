As in all turnover ratios, a high asset turnover is a desirable outcome as that would result from the effective use of available assets. Comparison between firms within an industry can be difficult due to the fact that the age of the assets used in the production of the sales may vary. This is a limitation caused by the use of historical costs, as opposed to current values for the productive assets. There is also a problem when comparing a capital-intensive firm to a labor-intensive one.

The important point to remember here is that the calculation of these ratios provides information about the subject company to the business valuator. The results of these calculations for the subject company must compare reasonably well to the calculations of those same ratios for potential comparable organization to remain on the list of possible guideline companies.

In the example, analysis of this nature, using quantitative factors, would suggest that Companies B and C may be comparable to the subject organization; thus, accepting them as potential guideline organizations. A similar analysis of key ratios should be performed for any company deemed to be a potential guideline. Such an exercise will help determine the range within which measurements for the closely held company should fall relative to the averages of the group of guideline companies.
