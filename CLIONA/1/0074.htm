The focus of the analysis of the historic profitability of the subject company is on risk. Two important questions to ask would be:

How has the company performed in the past?

Is that experience likely to continue in the future?

One of the analysis tools often introduced in a beginning managerial accounting course is that of the common sized statement. Such a review of the available income statements can provide a number of insights into the operations of the valued business. It is important to remember to remove any items from the historic data that are not likely to recur in the future before such a study is begun (normalization process).
