What is a Guideline Company?

A comparable company has been defined as one which has reasonable and justifiable similarity to the closely held business that is being valued. The issue of reasonable similarity has been addressed in two cases. Tallichet v. Commissioner, T.C. Memo 1974-255 (1974), indicated that there are “guideposts in determining comparability.” Estate of Victor B. Clarke, T.C. Memo 1976-328 (1976), stated “it is imperative that the characteristics of the subject company and the purported comparable company relevant to the question of value be isolated and examined so that significant comparison can be made.”

These cases take into account factors such as:

the products provided,

markets served,

size of the firm,

earnings,

capital structure,

dividend-paying capacity,

management capability, and

financing sources.

Justifiable similarity means that the valuator could defend his/her choice of guidelines to other informed individuals.
