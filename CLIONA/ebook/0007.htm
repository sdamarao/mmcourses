The History of Valuations

In the 1920s, the 18th Amendment was passed (prohibition), and businesses producing or serving alcoholic beverages—that were forced to close—had to calculate the value of their businesses in order to determine their losses for IRS purposes. Since there were numerous closely held organizations that would be reporting losses related to prohibition, the IRS provided valuation guidelines in a series of Appeals and Review Memorandums (ARMs) and Revenue Rulings. Revenue Ruling 59-60 presented a general approach for valuation. This ruling provided a general valuation method and list of factors to be considered in valuing shares of stock of closely held corporations for estate- and gift-tax purposes.
