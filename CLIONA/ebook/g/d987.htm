Business Segment

The following terms and descriptions of business segments are taken from FASB ASC 280-10-50 (Segment Reporting).

Operating segment. This is a component of an enterprise:

  • that engages in business activities where it earns revenues and incurs expenses,

  • whose operating results are regularly reviewed by chief operating decision makers, and

  • for which discrete financial information is available.

Certain operating segments may also be reportable segments (¶10).

Reportable segment. Operating segments that meet one of the following quantitative tests are reportable segments (¶18):

  • Segment revenue (both to external customers and intersegmental) is at least 10% of total revenue of all operating segments.

  • The absolute amount of segment profit (or loss) is at least 10% of all operating segments with a profit (or loss).

  • Operating segment assets are at least 10% of total assets.

Reportable segments must report their revenue, profit (or loss), assets, and other related items (¶27).

Enterprises must report the extent of reliance on major customers—those who comprise at least 10% of sales (¶39).
