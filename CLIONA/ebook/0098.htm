Guideline Public and Guideline Transaction Valuation Methods

Guideline Public Valuation Method

Once financial comparability is established and a group of guideline publicly traded companies have been chosen, the next step is to do a step-by-step comparison of the closely held firm with each of the guidelines to determine the relative strengths and weaknesses of the subject firm. Finally, if sufficient guideline companies have been found, the process culminates with the development of a “value measure” that can be applied to a specific variable of the closely held company to determine its estimated value. Such value measures are generally focused on some measure of earnings or invested capital. In our example, “earnings after tax” is used. Please note that in the example used that only two guideline firms are utilized. In practice, it would be necessary to have additional firms with appropriate comparability to the subject company.
