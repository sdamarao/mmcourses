Ratio Analysis

Continuing with our analysis of the dwindling list of possible guideline companies, size and ratio analysis can provide a further verification as to whether a particular publicly traded company or guideline transaction company can be a reasonably comparable.

example

Consider the following example. You have been tasked with finding guideline companies for a grocery store. Using just a few of the possible ratios that can be easily calculated, and dealing with grocery stores with up to $1 million in annual sales, you can narrow down the list of potential guideline firms. You put together a comparison of the sales levels of the subject company and three possible guidelines as shown in the following table:

Ratio  Closely Held Company Company A Company B Company C
Sales $ 475,000 $ 1,000,000 $ 625,000 $ 850,000
