So what definition of value should be used in a business valuation engagement? The answer to that is quite simple – That depends! There are no one-size-fits all situations in the field of business valuation. Different value definitions should potentially be used for different business valuations—depending upon the circumstances related to the particular engagement. In fact, one of the items that must be clearly defined in a business valuation report is the standard of value that is used. The definition of value, purpose of the valuation, and type of ownership interest will affect the selection of valuation methods. For example, in a divorce engagement, a company will be valued based upon relevant statues and case law for the particular state where the divorce is being litigated—it is not unusual for the courts to require a particular definition of value for a particular case. Thus, the definition of value used is especially critical to the valuation process. The standard of value is:

The identification of the type of value being utilized in a specific engagement; for example, fair market value, fair value, investment value. (International Glossary of Business Valuation Terms, June 2001)
