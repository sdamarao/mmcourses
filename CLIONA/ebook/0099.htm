example

Taking Companies 1 and 2, the following table discloses that they have market values of $16.2 million and $15.6 million, respectively. This market value is the price of their stock times the number of outstanding shares. This translates into a simple average market value to earnings ratio for the guideline firms of 40.5:1. (Company A: MV to EAT of 40 + Company B: MV to EAT of 41)/2

      Closely Held
Company		           Company 1       Company 2
1. Price per share       $ 9 per share     $ 13 per share
2. Number of shares outstanding       1.8 million      1.2 million
3. Market value of equity (1 × 2)        16.2 million      15.6 million
4. Earnings after tax (EAT) $ 300,000      405,000      380,000
5. Ratio of market value to EAT (3/4) 40.5      40      41

Applying this value measure to the profit-after-tax earnings of the closely held company results in an estimated fair market value of $12,150,000.

Fair Market Value = Earnings after tax × Average ratio of market value to EAT
= $ 300,000 × 40.5
= $ 12,150,000
