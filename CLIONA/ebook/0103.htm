Here we see that the estimated value for the first two measures is very similar but the value for the P/E ratio is significantly different. As a result, the analyst could consider that the Price/Earnings measure is not reliable to use in a valuation calculation for this particular industry.

If after determining several appropriate multiples, the results of a potential value for the subject company results in a wide range of results, the analyst might determine that the use of a guideline method is not appropriate since the wide dispersion of values cannot be easily explained nor reconciled. Delaware Chancery Court cited “Law of One Price”, concluding that a broad range of values resulting from comparables analysis to be too unreliable to be given significant weight. (Cede & Co. v. JRC Acquisition Corp., 2004 WL 286963 and U.S. Cellular Operating Co., 2005 WL 43994.) The Law of One Price is an economic term that states that in an efficient market all identical goods must have only one price, net of the price of transferring goods between the markets.
