The debt to equity ratio measures the relationship of debt to capital sources. The ratio of the dollars of debt per dollar of equity is determined. It shows the degree to which stockholders have a stake in the organization.

Debt to Equity = Total Debt/Total Stockholder Equity

The company's ability to meet long-term obligations is measured. From a creditor's point of view, the lower the ratio, the lower the risk of insolvency.

The times interest earned ratio measures the ability of the company to cover interest charges. This is important because, at minimum, a company would need to pay current interest charges.

Times Interest Earned = Net Income Before Interest and Taxes/Interest Expense
