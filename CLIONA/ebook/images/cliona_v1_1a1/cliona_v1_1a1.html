The three important components included in the definition of fair value that are important to understand are:

1. Price

2. Principal market

3. Market participants
2
Page 2

Price: The hypothetical transaction is assumed to have occurred in an orderly fashion. In other words, the holder of the asset or liability is able to take advantage of normal market activities for the transaction in question. The item being valued is considered to be exposed to the market for an appropriate amount of time prior to the measurement date. Furthermore, this is not a sale made under conditions of forced liquidation or distress.

Principal market: It is assumed that the hypothetical transaction occurred in either the principal market for such a transaction, or in the absence of such a market, the most advantageous market. Principal market is defined as the market where the holder of the asset or liability could find the greatest volume of asset sales or liability transfers similar to the item in question.

Most advantageous market is the one where the price received for the asset sale is maximized, or the transfer cost of the liability is minimized.

Market participants: The market participants are the seller (holder of the asset or liability being valued) and the buyer in the principal (or most advantageous) market for the asset or liability. It is assumed that the seller and buyer are independent, knowledgeable, and have both the ability and willingness to complete the transaction.
