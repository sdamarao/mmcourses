Profitability Ratios

Profitability is the measure of success in terms of income defined by varying means over a period of time. In analyzing profitability, earnings are compared to a base such as sales, productive assets, or equity. Increased profitability provides the potential for increased dividend payments and stock appreciation. Increased profitability also provides greater security for debt holders.

The asset turnover ratio

The asset turnover ratio indicates how many dollars of sales were created by each dollar of total assets. It helps to determine whether the available assets were used efficiently to create sales. The goal of management is to create the highest possible amount of sales per dollar of invested capital. Note that a reasonable range for this ratio may be determined for each industry.

Asset Turnover = Sales/Average Assets

The return on assets ratio

The return on assets ratio measures the productivity of assets in terms of producing income. This ratio depends upon the organization's ability to get a high profit from each sales dollar while creating high sales per dollar of invested capital. A consistently high ROA is an indication that management is making effective decisions, and that the organization is a “growth company.”

Return Assets = Income/Average Total Assets
