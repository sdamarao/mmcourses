Example

If a buyer was purchasing a dry cleaning business on a corner lot that he intended to continue operating, he would be concerned about the future value that could be created. He would be purchasing a going concern and would be most interested in the potential future income of the establishment.

If the buyer was purchasing this same business in order to obtain the land so that a parking ramp could be constructed after razing the current facility, he would be concerned about the future value that could be created by the proposed parking lot. In this case, the purchaser would be concerned about the liquidation value of the assets to be sold and the fair market value of the land.
