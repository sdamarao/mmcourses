Since it is often future benefits that are purchased when a business changes hands, it is important to look at the future earnings capacity for the subject company. Because conditions generally do not change quickly, the earnings of the past are often, by and large, a reliable guide to the profitability of the future.

Revenue Ruling 59-60 cautions not to resort to five- or ten-year averages without considering current trends. For example, if the sales of the main product produced by the subject company showed rapid growth in the first four years and for the next three years exhibited a leveling off of that growth, more weight should be put on the most recent years when determining the future earnings power of the organization. (Rev. Rul. 59-60, §4.02(d))
